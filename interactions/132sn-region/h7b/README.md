# H7B

`H7B` is a residual interaction used in the Chou-Warburton and Warburton-Towner A ~= 132 beta-decay studies. It is not documented here as a standalone KSHELL valence-space interaction file.

## Provenance

Chou and Warburton identify `H7B` as the interaction of Hosaka, Kubo, and Toki, based on the Paris nucleon-nucleon potential.

In the Chou-Warburton interaction construction, `H7B` is used to generate the TBMEs involving the added proton `0g9/2` orbit in the `KH4082` and `CW4082` spaces, together with the Coulomb interaction.

In the Chou-Warburton and Warburton-Towner beta-decay calculations, `H7B` is also used as a residual interaction for core-polarization correction factors.

## Notes

`H7B` has a stronger tensor component than `HBB` in the comparisons discussed by Warburton and Towner. The Warburton-Towner paper states that using `H7B` instead of `HBB` gives smaller rank-zero axial-charge matrix elements in their `133Sn` test.

This entry records the literature role of `H7B`; it does not include a raw interaction file.
