# HBB

`HBB` is a residual interaction used in A ~= 132 beta-decay core-polarization studies. It is documented here as a literature/provenance entry only; no raw interaction file is included.

## Provenance

Chou and Warburton state that A. Hosaka made a fit with the same potential form as `H7B` to the Bonn nucleon-nucleon G matrix. This interaction is designated `HBB`.

The source is cited as a private communication from A. Hosaka to B. A. Brown.

## Role In The A ~= 132 Papers

In Chou and Warburton, `HBB` is used as one of two residual interactions for core-polarization correction factors, alongside `H7B`.

In Warburton and Towner, final results are summarized using the `CW4082` shell-model interaction, meson-exchange corrections, and core polarization from `HBB`. Their comparison of `HBB` and `H7B` is used to estimate sensitivity to the residual interaction and tensor component.

## Notes

The Bonn potential has a tensor component about `25%` weaker than the Paris-potential interaction used for `H7B`, according to the Chou-Warburton and Warburton-Towner discussion.

This entry should not be treated as a reconstructable standalone interaction file. It records where the name `HBB` appears in the A ~= 132 interaction trail.
